The UK cost-of-living crisis

The UK cost-of-living crisis of 2022-25 was the sharpest squeeze on real household incomes since records began. UK CPI inflation peaked at 11.1% in October 2022, driven by a surge in wholesale energy prices following Russia's invasion of Ukraine and, to a lesser extent, by tight post-pandemic labour markets. Real household disposable income fell by around 2.3% in 2022-23, erasing a decade of earnings growth.

The government's response combined targeted and universal measures. The £400 Energy Bills Support Scheme was paid to all UK households in 2022-23, while the Energy Price Guarantee capped a typical household's annual gas and electricity bill at £2,500 from October 2022. Cost-of-living payments of £900 were made to more than 8 million low-income households. The Office for Budget Responsibility estimated the total fiscal cost at around £78 billion over two years.

(Source: adapted from Resolution Foundation & OBR reports, 2025)

The UK energy price cap

The UK default tariff cap ("energy price cap") was introduced by Ofgem in 2019 to protect households on standard variable tariffs from excessive pricing by energy suppliers. The cap sets the maximum price a supplier can charge per unit of gas and electricity; it is updated quarterly to reflect wholesale costs, network charges, and policy costs.

During the 2022-23 energy crisis, the cap rose steeply as wholesale prices spiked. From October 2022 the government temporarily froze consumer bills via the Energy Price Guarantee, absorbing the difference between the wholesale cost and the capped consumer price through subsidy.

Critics argue the cap dampens competition by narrowing differentials between suppliers and removing incentives to switch. Proponents argue it is essential to prevent consumer exploitation in a market with significant information asymmetry and switching inertia. The House of Lords Industry and Regulators Committee recommended in 2024 that Ofgem reform the cap to allow greater tariff differentiation and encourage customer engagement.

(Source: adapted from Ofgem & House of Lords committee reports, 2025)
