Question 3 — Total 5 marks

Indicative content (candidates may include any of the following — this list is not exhaustive and other valid points should be credited):

  • Higher policy rate reduces borrowing demand → AD shifts left.
  • Lower AD → lower equilibrium price level and lower output in short run.
  • Credibility effect: if anchoring is restored, inflation expectations fall further → SRAS eventually shifts right.
  • Short-run cost: recession risk; unemployment rises.
Answer: B
Taylor rule: policy rate responds to inflation deviation and output-gap deviation.

Question 4 — Total 5 marks

Indicative content (candidates may include any of the following — this list is not exhaustive and other valid points should be credited):

  • Currency depreciation raises imported input prices → SRAS shifts left.
  • Imported consumer goods more expensive → cost-push inflation.
  • In short run: higher price level and lower output.
  • Export-competitiveness gains partially offsetting (but slow).
Answer: A
Small open economies importing priced-in-foreign-currency final goods see the fastest and most complete pass-through.

Question 5 — Total 5 marks

Indicative content (candidates may include any of the following — this list is not exhaustive and other valid points should be credited):

  • Hot-money inflows finance current-account deficit but are volatile.
  • Sudden stop → currency crisis, sharp real depreciation, import compression.
  • External borrowing often in foreign currency → balance-sheet effects.
  • Productivity gains only if inflows finance productive investment.
Answer: C
A sudden stop forces rapid external rebalancing via depreciation and contractionary domestic policy.
