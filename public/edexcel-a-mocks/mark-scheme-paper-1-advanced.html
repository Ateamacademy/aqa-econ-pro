General Marking Guidance

All candidates must receive the same treatment. Examiners must mark the first candidate in exactly the same way as they mark the last. Mark schemes should be applied positively. Candidates must be rewarded for what they have shown they can do rather than penalised for omissions.

Crossed-out work should be marked unless the candidate has replaced it with an alternative response. All the marks on the mark scheme are designed to be awarded. Examiners should always award full marks if deserved, i.e. if the answer matches the mark scheme.

Question 2 — Total 5 marks

Indicative content (candidates may include any of the following — this list is not exhaustive and other valid points should be credited):

  • (a) Lerner Index = (P − MC) / P = (30,000 − 3,500) / 30,000 = 26,500 / 30,000 = 0.88.
  • (b) Lerner Index of 0.88 implies extremely high market power. Consistent with a near-monopoly; prices set well above competitive level.
Answer: B
Lerner = 0 → P = MC, the hallmark of perfect competition.

Question 3 — Total 5 marks

Indicative content (candidates may include any of the following — this list is not exhaustive and other valid points should be credited):

  • Export controls restrict supply of advanced chips to China → inward supply shift.
  • Within restricted market: price rises; quantity falls.
  • Dynamic effects: Chinese firms invest in domestic alternatives (Huawei Ascend) → eventual outward supply shift in rival markets.
  • Welfare: static loss to Chinese consumers; ambiguous long-run effect on global innovation.
Answer: C
Strategic trade theory: export controls justified when positive spillovers (innovation, employment) from strategic industry accrue to exporting country.

Question 4 — Total 5 marks

Indicative content (candidates may include any of the following — this list is not exhaustive and other valid points should be credited):

  • Rising compute costs shift AC and MC curves upward on firm's cost-revenue diagram.
  • Break-even point rises → higher revenue threshold for new entrants.
  • Barrier to entry increases → concentration of frontier AI in a few well-capitalised firms.
  • Incumbency advantage rises; Nvidia's pricing power is partially a reflection of this.
Answer: B
Large MES relative to demand → few firms can operate at efficient scale → natural monopoly or tight oligopoly.

Question 5 — Total 5 marks

Indicative content (candidates may include any of the following — this list is not exhaustive and other valid points should be credited):

  • AI compute has positive externalities: basic research, workforce training, ecosystem effects.
  • Private compute investment under-provides public-good aspects → market failure.
  • Subsidy internalises externality and improves allocative efficiency.
  • Evaluation: risk of subsidy capture by incumbents; difficulty of targeting MEB.
Answer: B
Pigouvian subsidy set equal to marginal external benefit at socially optimal quantity.
