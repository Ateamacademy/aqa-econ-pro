Extract A
Password-sharing crackdown tests customer loyalty

Netflix ended password-sharing across households in the UK in 2023, forcing users to either subscribe individually or pay an “extra member” fee of £4.99 per month. Initial churn was higher than expected, but subscriber numbers recovered within two quarters. Netflix has since launched an ad-supported tier at £4.99 per month, attracting price-sensitive consumers.

Content spending in the global streaming market reached \$140 billion in 2024, with Netflix alone spending \$17 billion on new content. Production costs for original content have risen sharply — the cost of producing a high-end drama series has roughly doubled since 2019, driven by a “talent war” between streamers. Some analysts argue the industry has entered a “winner-takes-most” phase where smaller platforms struggle to justify content investment.

Disney+ and Paramount+ have begun bundling their services. In the US, Disney+ and Hulu merged their content libraries in 2024. In the UK, Sky has responded by integrating streaming apps directly into its Sky Glass and Sky Stream hardware.

(Source: adapted from https://www.ft.com/content/streaming-economics-2024)

Extract B
Content is king, but distribution is queen

The streaming market shows features of monopolistic competition (many differentiated products, low switching costs) but also of oligopoly (top 4 = 84% of subscribers). Consumer surveys show 62% of UK households now subscribe to two or more streaming services, up from 37% in 2020. However, 18% of subscribers in 2024 said they planned to cancel at least one service within 12 months, suggesting the market is reaching saturation.

The UK regulator Ofcom has expressed concern about the “algorithmic recommendation” systems used by streamers, which it argues may harm smaller UK production companies by favouring in-house originals. Ofcom is investigating whether prominence obligations should be placed on streamers similar to those required on traditional broadcasters.

(Source: adapted from https://www.ofcom.org.uk/streaming-market-review-2024)
