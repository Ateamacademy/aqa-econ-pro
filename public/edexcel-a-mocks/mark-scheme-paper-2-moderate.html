QAnswer / Indicative contentMarksAO

Question 7 (EITHER) — Multi-year freeze of personal allowance

QAnswer / Indicative contentMarksAO
7Evaluate macroeconomic effects:
  • Fiscal drag: nominal wage rises pull more workers into tax / higher tax bands; effective tax burden rises without headline-rate changes.
  • AD effect: lower real disposable income → lower C → AD shifts left.
  • Fiscal position: higher tax receipts → lower borrowing; may stabilise debt-to-GDP ratio.
  • Labour supply: reduced marginal return to work may reduce hours supplied at the margin; but income effect may work opposite way.
  • Income distribution: regressive relative to index-linked allowance; disproportionate impact on real wage workers.
  • Inflation: reducing AD pressure helps BoE's 2% target.
  • Growth: dampens short-run growth; trade-off with fiscal sustainability.
  • Political economy: 'stealth tax' perceptions damage consent for tax system.
  • Evaluation: depends on cyclical state, relative elasticities; preferable to rate rises in stable-tax-system context, but regressive.

Question 8 (OR) — Stronger BoE inflation-targeting mandate

QAnswer / Indicative contentMarksAO
8Evaluate claim:
  • Current mandate: symmetric 2% CPI target; lexicographic priority of inflation over output.
  • Arguments FOR strengthening: anchor for inflation expectations; CB independence; time-inconsistency protection; credibility benefits (lower risk premium on gilts).
  • Arguments AGAINST: current mandate already strong; dual mandate (Fed) models show flexibility can improve outcomes (Svensson, flexible IT).
  • Trade-off: strong focus on inflation during 2022–23 meant output near-flat and labour market weakening; flexibility would have allowed earlier easing.
  • Alternative frameworks: nominal GDP targeting, average inflation targeting (Fed 2020), price-level targeting.
  • Supply-shock problem: pure IT pushes CB to tighten into supply shocks; dual mandate allows 'looking through'.
  • Low inflation doesn't guarantee low unemployment (cf. Phillips curve shifts).
  • Evaluation: strengthening inflation priority likely counter-productive; improvements in communication and flexibility more valuable than rigidification.
