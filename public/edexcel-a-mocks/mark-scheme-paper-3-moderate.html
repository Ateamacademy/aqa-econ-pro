Indicative content (candidates may include any of the following — this list is not exhaustive and other valid points should be credited):

  • Windfall taxes capture supernormal profits from exogenous price shocks; revenue funds targeted support.
  • Case for: fairness; fiscal feasibility; economic efficiency (supernormal profit has no allocative role).
  • Case against: risk of reducing investment incentive; capital-flight risk; double taxation if firms already pay corporation tax.
  • UK EPL (Energy Profits Levy) example: raised ≈ £26bn but with investment-allowance carve-outs.
  • International: Italian, Spanish, EU-level windfall-tax frameworks.
  • Evaluation: depends on design (one-off vs permanent), investment-allowance treatment, global coordination.

  • Micro consequences: price signals distorted → weaker incentive for energy efficiency investment; allocation of scarce energy impaired; consumer surplus temporarily supported at social expense.
  • Macro consequences: fiscal cost (£78bn) → higher debt/GDP; crowding-out of other public investment.
  • Inflation suppression: subsidy lowers headline CPI temporarily but transfers the cost to the public finances.
  • Distributional: universal schemes benefit the rich; targeted transfers are more efficient.

  • International examples: France TRVE, Spain Iberian exception, German gas-price brake.
  • Evaluation: one-off targeted support is more efficient than universal subsidies; lesson for future shocks.
